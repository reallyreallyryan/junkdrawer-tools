What is LLMS.txt?

LLMS.txt is a standardized file format that helps AI search engines (also called Large Language Models or LLMs) better understand and categorize your website's content. Think of it as a sitemap specifically designed for AI consumption.

The file follows a simple markdown structure that organizes your website's pages into logical categories, making it easy for AI engines to:

  • Understand what your website offers
  • Categorize your content accurately
  • Provide relevant recommendations to users
  • Include your content in AI-generated responses

How LLMS.txt Files Work

An LLMS.txt file is placed at your website's root directory (yoursite.com/llms.txt) and follows a structured format:

# Your Website Name > Brief description of what your website offers ## Services - [Service Name](URL): Description of what this service provides - [Another Service](URL): Clear benefit-focused description ## About - [Company Story](URL): Learn about our mission and values - [Our Team](URL): Meet the experts behind our success ## Resources - [Blog](URL): Industry insights and expert guidance - [Case Studies](URL): Real results from real clients

This structure allows AI engines to quickly parse your content and understand the relationship between different pages on your site.

Common LLMS.txt Implementation Mistakes

While the concept is simple, many websites make critical errors when creating their LLMS.txt files:

1. Poor Categorization

Throwing all pages into generic categories like "Pages" doesn't help AI engines understand your content structure. Use descriptive categories that reflect your business model.

2. Generic Descriptions

Descriptions like "Learn more about our services" don't provide value. Instead, focus on specific benefits: "Reduce operational costs through automation consulting."

3. Including Non-Essential Pages

Your LLMS.txt file should focus on pages that provide value to users. Privacy policies, terms of service, and other legal pages typically don't belong in your LLMS.txt file.

4. Inconsistent Formatting

AI engines expect consistent markdown formatting. Inconsistent structures can confuse parsing algorithms.
